DEPARTMENT: Computer and Information Systems Engineering
UNIVERSITY: NED University of Engineering & Technology

--- HISTORY AND ESTABLISHMENT ---
- July 1983: NED University introduced the four-year Bachelor of Engineering (Computer Systems) program.
- 1986: The Department of Electrical Engineering was renamed to the Department of Electrical and Computer Systems Engineering.
- July 1997: The Department was bifurcated to create a separate Department of Computer Systems Engineering, recognizing the field's growing importance for the 21st century.
- 2000: The department was renamed to its current title: Department of Computer and Information Systems Engineering.

--- DEPARTMENTAL ORGANIZATION ---
- Leadership: The department is led by a Chairman.
- Reporting: The Chairman reports to the Dean of the Faculty of Electrical & Computer Engineering (ECE).
- Authority: The Chairman exercises administrative and financial powers as per University Statutes, Regulations, and delegated authority.
- Current Chairman: Prof. Dr. Muhammad Ali Ismail.
- Faculty: Under the chairman's guidance, a team of experienced faculty members works to achieve excellence in academics and research.

--- RESEARCH FIELDS ---
The department is active in the following key research areas:
- High Performance Computing
- Artificial Intelligence and Robotics
- Software Defined Communication Systems
- Computer Vision & Image Processing
- Embedded System Design
- Internet of Things (IoT) and Web of Things
- Multicore Architecture Design
- VLSI Design and Testing
- Distributed Systems
- Computer Networks

--- RESEARCH CENTRES ---
The department hosts and is associated with several national research centers:
1. High Performance Computing Centre
2. National Center in Big Data and Cloud Computing
3. National Center for Artificial Intelligence (Smart City Lab and Neuromorphic Lab)
4. National Centre for Cyber Security (comprising the Internet Security Lab, Digital Forensic Lab, and Quantum Computing Lab)
